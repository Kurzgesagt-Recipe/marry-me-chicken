Marry Me Chicken Recipe

Home Ingredients Preparation Gallery Contact

Introduction

This recipe holds a special place in my heart because it was the first real recipe I attempted to cook. I chose to make this dish for my now wife to showcase my cooking abilities. Initially, I was content with eating chicken and rice, but this recipe has become a favorite for us to cook together. The creamy, savory sauce infused with sun-dried tomatoes and fresh herbs makes it a standout dish.

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp. extra-virgin olive oil, divided
  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (8-oz each)
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp. fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 tsp. crushed red pepper flakes
  • 3/4 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup chopped sun-dried tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup finely grated Parmesan
  • Torn fresh basil for serving

Preparation Steps

  1. Season chicken breasts with salt and pepper.
  2. Brown in olive oil over medium heat; set aside.
  3. In the same pan, sauté garlic, thyme, and red pepper flakes.
  4. Add chicken broth and sun-dried tomatoes; simmer until thickened.
  5. Stir in heavy cream and Parmesan until a creamy sauce forms.
  6. Return chicken to the pan; simmer until cooked through.
  7. Garnish with fresh basil before serving.

Cooking Tips

To ensure your Marry Me Chicken turns out perfect, make sure not to overcook the chicken breasts. Use a meat thermometer to check for doneness. The internal temperature should reach 165°F. You can also pound the chicken breasts to an even thickness to ensure they cook evenly.

Variations

You can customize this recipe by adding different herbs such as rosemary or oregano. If you like a bit more heat, increase the amount of crushed red pepper flakes. For a richer flavor, you can use sun-dried tomatoes packed in oil.

Serving Suggestions

Serve this dish with a side of pasta, mashed potatoes, or a fresh green salad. The creamy sauce is delicious over pasta or rice. Don't forget to pair it with a nice glass of white wine!
